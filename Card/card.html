PV Sindhu crashes out of China Open

world champion and title favourite PV Sindhu crashed out of the China Open Super 1000 tournament after suffering a pre-quarterfinals defeat to Thailand’s Pornpawee Chochuwong in the women’s singles on Wednesday. India’s top shuttler Sindhu lost to Thailand’s Pornpawee Chochuwong 21-12, 13-21, 19-21 in a duel which lasted for more than 50 minutes.

Sushil Kumar knocked out

India’s Parveen Rana lost to Changjae Sue 12-1 after having won his first-round match against Korea’s Changjae Sue 12-1 by technical superiority in 92Kg bout. Earlier, Sushil Kumar, Sumit Malik and Karan Mor lost their opening bout on Day 7 at World Championships. Parveen successfully completed two takedowns to take a 4-0 lead going into the break. In round 2, Parveen counter-attacked to take a huge lead of 12-1 lead and win his opening bout.

Bajrang Punia stages dramatic comeback to win

Bajrang Punia staged another late comeback in the bronze medal match at the Wrestling World Championship on Friday, coming back from 0-6 down to win 8-7 against Mongolia’s Tulga Tumur Ochir. This is his third medal in the World Championships.
